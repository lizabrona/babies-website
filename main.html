Being a parent

Being a parent is the best thing ever, but it is also the biggest challenge. Sometimes it seems overwhelming, but you can handle it. Just be patient, trust your instincts but in the same time try to learn as much as you can. Some mothers say: "I don't read parenting literature, I'm just following my instincts." Well this is good, but what you instincts know about heathy eating or child emergency medical treatment?. On the other hand you don't need to follow all the instructions given in parenting books, use your own judgment, but you need to be as well informed as you can. This is your child, and you are responsible for his or hers heath and future. Here are some useful links:

  • www.netmums.com
  • www.parenting.co.uk

Is your child potty trained?


I'm proud to say that my daughter started to use potty when she was 1.5 years old. Some parents say that their children began to use potty by themselves. Well, miracles happen... If you don't won't to wait for a miracle, here are some tips:


  • Don't use nappies during the day.
  • Put a potty in a visible place.
  • Don't criticize.
  • Be patient.
